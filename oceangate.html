Virtual OceanGate
IN PROGRESS

Summary:

Virtual OceanGate is a digital replica of an OceanGate manned submersible, created in the Second Life virtual world. The purpose of this project was to demonstrate how immersive virtual environments can be used to educate, train, and inspire people about real world tools and their applications.

My role
My role was experience producer, content writer, and presenter, and I collaborated with 3D artists, coders, video producers, and front-end developers who helped bring the project and our video presentation to life.

Why does this matter?

Sending people out in submersibles for training and demonstration purposes is a time-consuming and expensive process. With an effective virtual simulation, OceanGate could reduce the costs of training, create better-prepared pilots, and more closely monitor which areas of preparedness need attention before high-stakes missions in the physical ocean.

Our Methods:

As an experience producer and content writer, it was up to me to figure out how to make our teaching tool both engaging and educational. Because this was a class on Immersive Environments, I first chose to narrow down some key factors of immersion that we would want to build as our minimum viable presentation.

According to Bailenson and Scarborough (2014), the three characteristics of virtual presence are self-presence, social presence, and spatial presence. In other words, to make someone feel virtually immersed, we would have to ensure that users maintained their sense of self while in our submersible, create a rich world with sound and visual artifacts on all sides, and give users a social framework in which to feel like what they did and said mattered.

Spatial and self-presence were built into our digital, controllable submersible as an artifact, but social presence and engagement among 40 audience-members was harder to engineer.
