Virtual OceanGate
IN PROGRESS

Summary:
Virtual OceanGate is a digital replica of an OceanGate manned submersible, created in the Second Life virtual world. The purpose of this project was to demonstrate how immersive virtual environments can be used to educate, train, and inspire people about real world tools and their applications.

MY ROLE
My role was experience producer, content writer, and presenter, and I collaborated with 3D artists, coders, video producers, and front-end developers who helped bring the project and our video presentation to life.

Why does this matter?
Sending people out in submersibles for training and demonstration purposes is a time-consuming and expensive process. With an effective virtual simulation, OceanGate could reduce the costs of training, create better-prepared pilots, and more closely monitor which areas of preparedness need attention before high-stakes missions in the physical ocean.

Our Methods:
Identify and narrow down key components which make for an immersive and engaging experience. Narrowing down key components of what makes an experience both immersive and compelling, while figuring out how we could provide value both to OceanGate and to our own classmates/audience who would participate in our 10 minute demonstration. We found excerpts from readings and took inspiration from other virtual worlds to create spatial, social, and self presence by creating a rich environment, complete with X, Y, Z…and
As such, rather than simply recreate their product, we designed an environment in which the underwater submersible could be used to carry out specified tasks.This will highlight the spatial immersion of the given environment and allow users to see how the Cyclops will improve tomorrows underwater excursions.
Self presence - “the feeling that one’s avatar is more than a mechanism to interact with the virtual environment…it is an extension of the self” – the ability to earn lindens (second life money) and both place and see their virtual avatar inside the submersible

Social Presence – the feeling of competition with others to get the treasure chests and discover the environment
Spatial presence – a rich world with sounds, physics, and artifacts to discover on all sides. Engagement – rewards, scavenger hunt, hidden treasures, and the feeling of urgency created by the pipeline disaster we had to stop, using the submersible. Not only did we create a submersible but we created a story and a compelling reason to use it.
